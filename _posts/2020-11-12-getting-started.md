**## Why did I create this blog?**

I’ve asked this question over and over in the 2+ years since I purchased the domain [jtsmith.me](~https://jtsmith.me~), and it’s just one of the many questions I’ve had to answer since then. This blog post is the answer to the final question in that series, but let quickly walk through each step.

**### Why did you buy a custom domain?**

The idea came as I was diving into “tech twitter” for the first time. “Build your brand”, “Learn to write”, and “Catalog your work” were some of the headlines I would consistently see as I started to follow more of the big names in tech. “Yeah, I *_should_* do that” I thought to myself, but of course I couldn’t get started without my own domain!

**### You have a domain, but where are you going to host your site?**

One of the first justifications I had for creating my own blog was to learn some AWS tools in a practical way. The concept of a [static site](~https://aws.amazon.com/getting-started/hands-on/host-static-website/~) was new to me, but many had already paved the way, and built tooling, to make hosting a static site on AWS easy. Registering a domain, setting up Route53 (everyone’s favorite database), and storing the site artifacts in S3 are some of the core features this method uses. It’s very easy to do and I highly recommend checking it out if you need an easy way to host a site. I ran my site like this for some time and with the traffic I was getting (see: none) it was costing me $0.20/month. Being as that was just too expensive for my tastes, I continued looking for an alternative.

I tried out [Netlify](~https://www.netlify.com/~) but quickly determined it was overkill for my needs.

Finally, I discovered [Github Pages](~https://pages.github.com/~), which was even easier and free! My site is still running on Github Pages and you can find the source [here](~https://github.com/SmithJohnTaylor/SmithJohnTaylor.github.io~).

**### Domain :white_check_mark:, now what to choose for a theme…**

I’ve avoided CSS for a long time and I’ve only worked with it out of necessity for my professional career. The thought of doing it for fun on a personal project was very off-putting and directly influenced this blog sitting idly for some time. I mean, I’d rather paint grass or watch ceilings grow. So many options out there, so many different tools to learn, so many new languages to avoid, I just couldn’t bring myself to do it. Until…I found [Beautiful Jekyll](https://beautifuljekyll.com/)! It doesn’t get much easier than this and the blog is happily running on it. It allows me to focus on the blog and content and continue to avoid my weaknesses.

**### Now the site is up and running, what in the world are you going to put on it?**

This. This is the proverbial “million dollar question”. Nearly as long as I spent determining how to theme this site, I’ve contemplated what to put on it. What’s worse, blog aside, I’ve struggled with this question as it pertains my life and career. I’ve had this mindset of “only learn what’s helpful for you to learn” and that quickly becomes “what’s helpful to learn?” I don’t think I’ll have the answer to this question soon, or ever, but I need to start somewhere. So…

**### What’s next?**

I’m going to continue to work and learn and use blog posts to ensure I understand what it is I’m learning by turning thoughts into physical outputs. In the future, I hope this site is full of blogs of my experiences, demos of tech I’ve worked on, and lessons learned.

Thanks for reading.
