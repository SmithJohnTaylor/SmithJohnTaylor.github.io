## Why am I writing this post? 
I've asked this question over and over again to myself in the 2+ years since I purchased the domain (jtsmith.me)[https://jtsmith.me], and it's just one of the many questions I've had to answer for myself since that time. This blog post is the answer to the final question in that series, but let me break them down to show the mental journey I've traveled to this point.

### Why did you buy a custom domain?
The idea first came to me as I was diving into "tech twitter" for the first time. "Build your brand", "Learn to write", and "Catalog your work" were some of the headlines I would consistenly see as I started to follow more and more of the big names in tech. "Yeah, I _should_ do that" I thought to myself, but of course I couldn't get started without my own domain!

### You have a domain, but where are you going to host your site?
One of the first justifications I had for creating my own blog was to learn some AWS tools in a practical way. The concept of a (static site)[https://aws.amazon.com/getting-started/hands-on/host-static-website/] was new to me, but many had already paved the way, and built tooling, to make hosting a static site on AWS easy. Registering a domain, setting up Route53 (everyone's favorite database), and storing the site artifacts in S3 are some of the core features this method uses. It's very easy to do and I highly recommend checking it out if you need an easy way to host a site. 
I ran my site like this for some time and with the traffic I was getting (see: none) it was costing me $0.20/month. Being as that was just too expensive for my tastes, I was looking for an alternative and discovered (Github Pages)[https://pages.github.com/], which was even easier and free! My site is still running on Github Pages and you can find the source (here)[https://github.com/SmithJohnTaylor/SmithJohnTaylor.github.io].
